## Cost Components

### Total Landed Cost

```
Product Cost:
+ Purchase price
+ Freight/shipping inbound
+ Import duties
+ Insurance
= FOB cost

+ Warehousing allocation
+ Handling costs
+ Quality checking
= Total landed cost
```

### Operating Cost Allocation

```
Per-unit Operating Costs:
- Payment processing: 2-3%
- Platform fees: 2-5%
- Returns allowance: 2-5%
- Packaging: $0.50-$3.00
- Marketing allocation: 5-15%
- Customer service: 1-2%
```

### True Profit Calculation

```
Selling Price: $50.00
- Cost of Goods: $20.00
= Gross Profit: $30.00 (60% margin)

- Payment Processing (3%): $1.50
- Platform Fees (2%): $1.00
- Packaging: $1.50
- Shipping (if absorbed): $8.00
- Returns (5%): $2.50
- Marketing (10%): $5.00
= Net Contribution: $10.50 (21%)
```

## Competitive Positioning

### Price Position Strategy

| Position | vs Market | Margin Trade-off |
|----------|-----------|------------------|
| Price Leader | -10-20% | Low margin, high volume |
| Competitive | ±5% | Balanced |
| Premium | +10-20% | High margin, lower volume |
| Luxury | +30%+ | Maximum margin, niche |

### Position by Business

**BOO:** Competitive to Premium
- Match health food stores
- Below specialty organic stores
- Above mass market

**Teelixir:** Premium to Luxury
- Above competitors
- Value justified by quality
- Wholesale maintains brand value

**Elevate:** Competitive Wholesale
- Match major distributors
- Volume incentives
- Value-added services

**RHF:** Competitive Local
- Match local markets
- Beat supermarkets on quality
- Convenience premium for delivery

## Margin Optimization Levers

### Increase Margins

| Lever | Impact | Difficulty |
|-------|--------|------------|
| Price increase | High | Medium |
| Cost negotiation | Medium | Medium |
| Bundle creation | Medium | Low |
| Premium positioning | High | High |
| Exclusive products | High | Medium |
| Reduce returns | Medium | Medium |

### Protect Margins

| Threat | Mitigation |
|--------|------------|
| Competitor price drops | Differentiate, bundle |
| Cost increases | Pass through, negotiate |
| Promotions eroding | Limit frequency, depth |
| Returns | Better descriptions, photos |
| Fraud | Verification, limits |

## Seasonal Margin Adjustments

### High Season (Higher Margins OK)

- New Year wellness (Jan)
- Winter immunity (Jun-Aug)
- Pre-holiday (Nov)

### Low Season (Protect Volume)

- Post-holiday (Feb)
- Mid-year (Apr-May)
- Spring shoulder (Sep)
