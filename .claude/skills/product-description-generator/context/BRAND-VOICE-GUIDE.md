# Brand Voice Guide

## Buy Organics Online (BOO)

### Brand Personality
- **Trustworthy** - We're experts you can rely on
- **Supportive** - We help you on your wellness journey
- **Knowledgeable** - We understand organic and health products
- **Accessible** - Premium doesn't mean pretentious

### Voice Characteristics
| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Tone | Warm, informative, encouraging |
| Language | Clear, educational, jargon-free |
| Perspective | "We" and "you" - conversational |
| Expertise | Confident but not condescending |

### Writing Do's
- Lead with benefits to the customer
- Explain what makes products special
- Use Australian spelling and terms
- Include serving/usage suggestions
- Mention certifications prominently
- Connect products to lifestyle goals

### Writing Don'ts
- Don't use medical claims
- Don't be overly salesy or pushy
- Don't assume prior knowledge
- Don't use complex scientific terms without explanation
- Don't copy supplier descriptions verbatim

### Example Phrases
- "Nourish your body with..."
- "A natural choice for..."
- "Sourced from trusted suppliers..."
- "Supporting your wellness journey..."
- "Quality you can trust..."

### Category-Specific Notes

**Superfoods:**
- Focus on nutritional density
- Mention versatility (smoothies, baking, etc.)
- Highlight sourcing quality

**Supplements:**
- Lead with traditional use
- Include required disclaimers
- Be specific about form (capsules, powder, etc.)

**Pantry Items:**
- Emphasize purity and ingredients
- Suggest recipe ideas
- Note storage requirements

**Personal Care:**
- Highlight natural/organic ingredients
- Mention what's NOT in it (no parabens, etc.)
- Describe sensory experience (scent, texture)

## Teelixir

### Brand Personality
- **Expert** - Deep knowledge of tonic herbs
- **Passionate** - Genuine enthusiasm for wellness
- **Premium** - Quality-focused, not mass-market
- **Mystical** - Honoring ancient traditions

### Voice Characteristics
| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Tone | Inspiring, knowledgeable, passionate |
| Language | Elevated but accessible, wellness-focused |
| Perspective | Educational expert sharing wisdom |
| Expertise | Deep authority on functional mushrooms/adaptogens |

### Writing Do's
- Reference traditional use and history
- Explain dual-extraction and quality processes
- Use proper terminology (adaptogens, tonic herbs)
- Connect to modern wellness goals
- Highlight potency and bioavailability
- Create desire for transformation

### Writing Don'ts
- Don't make therapeutic claims
- Don't oversimplify (audience is knowledgeable)
- Don't use generic health food language
- Don't ignore sourcing/quality story
- Don't be boring or clinical

### Key Terminology
- **Adaptogens** - Help body adapt to stress
- **Tonic herbs** - Traditional herbs for vitality
- **Dual-extracted** - Water and alcohol extraction
- **Bioavailable** - Body can absorb and use
- **Qi** - Vital energy (Traditional Chinese Medicine)
- **Jing** - Essence/life force
- **Shen** - Spirit/consciousness

### Example Phrases
- "Harness the power of..."
- "Used for centuries in traditional medicine..."
- "Support your body's natural ability to..."
- "Experience the transformative benefits..."
- "Potent, pure, and powerful..."

### Product Category Notes

**Mushroom Extracts:**
- Highlight specific mushroom benefits
- Mention extraction ratio (e.g., 10:1)
- Note fruiting body vs mycelium

**Adaptogen Blends:**
- Explain synergy between ingredients
- Suggest use cases (energy, calm, focus)
- Position as daily ritual

**Single Herbs:**
- Deep dive on traditional use
- Mention origin and sourcing
- Explain preparation method

## Elevate Wholesale

### Brand Personality
- **Professional** - Business-to-business expertise
- **Supportive** - Partner in your success
- **Reliable** - Consistent quality and supply
- **Value-focused** - Help you maximize margins

### Voice Characteristics
| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Tone | Professional, helpful, business-like |
| Language | Clear, efficient, benefit-focused |
| Perspective | Business partner perspective |
| Expertise | Retail and wholesale expertise |

### Writing Do's
- Focus on retail benefits (margins, turnover)
- Mention customer demand/popularity
- Include practical details (shelf life, MOQ)
- Highlight marketing support available
- Use business metrics where relevant
- Position as partnership opportunity

### Writing Don'ts
- Don't use consumer marketing language
- Don't ignore commercial realities
- Don't be overly casual
- Don't forget decision-makers are businesses
- Don't skip practical ordering information

### Example Phrases
- "A proven seller that delivers..."
- "Stock your shelves with customer favourites..."
- "Competitive wholesale pricing..."
- "Marketing support included..."
- "Partner with us for..."

### Key Information to Include
- Wholesale price / RRP
- Margin potential
- Minimum order quantity
- Shelf life
- Best-selling status
- Marketing materials available
- Reorder frequency (if known)

## Red Hill Fresh

### Brand Personality
- **Local** - Proudly Mornington Peninsula
- **Fresh** - Quality you can taste
- **Community** - Supporting local farmers
- **Family** - Wholesome, accessible

### Voice Characteristics
| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Tone | Friendly, warm, authentic |
| Language | Simple, honest, down-to-earth |
| Perspective | Neighbor sharing good finds |
| Expertise | Local knowledge, farm connections |

### Writing Do's
- Mention local sourcing when applicable
- Include farm/producer names when known
- Emphasize freshness and seasonality
- Connect to local community
- Use friendly, conversational tone
- Suggest family-friendly uses

### Writing Don'ts
- Don't use corporate language
- Don't be impersonal
- Don't ignore the local story
- Don't use generic supermarket descriptions
- Don't forget seasonal availability notes

### Example Phrases
- "Straight from local farms..."
- "Fresh from the Mornington Peninsula..."
- "Supporting local growers..."
- "A family favourite..."
- "Picked fresh and delivered to you..."

### Category Notes

**Fresh Produce:**
- Mention farm/grower if known
- Note seasonal availability
- Suggest storage tips
- Include recipe ideas

**Local Products:**
- Tell the producer's story
- Highlight what makes them special
- Connect to local community

**Pantry Items:**
- Focus on quality and taste
- Mention local partnerships
- Suggest uses and pairings

## Cross-Brand Guidelines

### Universal Do's
- Write for humans first, SEO second
- Use active voice
- Break up long text with bullets/headers
- Include clear calls to action
- Proofread everything

### Universal Don'ts
- Don't make unsubstantiated claims
- Don't use ALL CAPS for emphasis
- Don't use excessive exclamation marks!!!
- Don't plagiarize competitor descriptions
- Don't forget mobile readers (short paragraphs)

### Australian English
- Use Australian spelling (colour, favour, organise)
- Use metric measurements
- Reference Australian seasons correctly
- Use local terminology (capsicums not peppers)
