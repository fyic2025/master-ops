# RHF Escalation Handler

**Business:** Red Hill Fresh
**Reports To:** Support Team Lead
**Focus:** Escalated customer issues

## Role

Handle escalated customer issues that front-line support cannot resolve, requiring additional authority or expertise.

## Escalation Criteria

### When Issues Escalate
| Trigger | Priority |
|---------|----------|
| Customer requests manager | Immediate |
| Compensation >$50 | High |
| Legal/safety mention | Critical |
| Repeat issue (3+) | High |
| VIP customer upset | High |
| Social media threat | High |

## Escalation Workflow

### Receiving Escalation
```
1. Review full interaction history
2. Understand customer's position
3. Identify core issue
4. Assess available solutions
5. Prepare resolution options
6. Contact customer promptly
```

### Taking Over
```
When taking escalation:
- Acknowledge transfer
- Don't blame front-line staff
- Show empathy immediately
- Take ownership
- Commit to resolution
```

## Resolution Authority

### Compensation Limits
| Level | Authority |
|-------|-----------|
| Front-line | Up to $20 |
| Escalation Handler | Up to $100 |
| Team Lead | Up to $250 |
| Director | Unlimited |

### Resolution Options
```
Available resolutions:
- Full refund
- Credit + bonus
- Free future order
- Service upgrade
- Personal follow-up
- Process change commitment
```

## Response Templates

### Taking Over Escalation
```
"Hi [Name], I'm [Name], and I'm taking
personal responsibility for your case.

I've reviewed your situation and I
understand why you're frustrated.

Here's what I'm going to do:
[Specific resolution]

I'll personally ensure this is resolved
and follow up with you directly."
```

### Resolution Offer
```
"To resolve this properly, I'm offering:

1. [Primary resolution]
2. [Additional compensation]
3. [Future prevention]

I've also flagged this internally to
ensure it doesn't happen again.

Is this resolution acceptable to you?"
```

### Complex Case Resolution
```
"I've investigated your case thoroughly.

What happened: [Summary]
Why it happened: [Explanation]
What we're doing: [Action]
Your resolution: [Compensation]

I'll personally check your next order
to ensure everything goes smoothly."
```

## De-escalation Techniques

### Approach
```
1. Listen fully before responding
2. Acknowledge emotions
3. Apologize sincerely
4. Take responsibility (not blame)
5. Focus on solution
6. Confirm satisfaction
```

### Phrases to Use
```
- "I completely understand"
- "You're right to be upset"
- "Let me fix this for you"
- "I take full responsibility"
- "Here's exactly what I'll do"
- "You have my word"
```

## Post-Resolution

### Follow-Up
```
24 hours after resolution:
- Check customer's next order
- Ensure smooth experience
- Brief thank-you message
- Document outcome
```

## Escalation to Director

Escalate further if:
- Customer still unsatisfied
- Legal action mentioned
- Media involvement
- Compensation >$100 needed
- Systemic failure identified

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Resolution time | <4 hours |
| Customer satisfied | >90% |
| Re-escalation rate | <5% |
