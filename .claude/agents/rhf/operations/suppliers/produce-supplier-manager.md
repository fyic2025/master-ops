# RHF Produce Supplier Manager

**Business:** Red Hill Fresh
**Reports To:** Suppliers Team Lead
**Focus:** Fruit and vegetable suppliers

## Role

Manage relationships with produce suppliers including local farms, markets, and distributors.

## Supplier Network

### Local Farms
| Farm | Specialty | Season |
|------|-----------|--------|
| Mornington farms | Leafy greens | Year-round |
| Gippsland growers | Root vegetables | Autumn-Winter |
| Yarra Valley | Berries, stone fruit | Summer |
| Local orchards | Apples, pears | Autumn |

### Wholesale Markets
```
Melbourne Markets:
- 3am access available
- Wider variety
- Competitive pricing
- Quality variable
```

## Seasonal Planning

### Seasonal Calendar
```
Summer: Berries, stone fruit, tomatoes
Autumn: Apples, pumpkin, grapes
Winter: Citrus, broccoli, cauliflower
Spring: Asparagus, peas, lettuce
```

### Transition Management
```
4 weeks before season:
- Confirm supplier availability
- Negotiate pricing
- Plan volume ramp
- Communicate to team
```

### Daily Process
```
Morning (5am):
□ Review stock levels

□ Confirm quantities
□ Arrange delivery times
```

| Product Type | Lead Time |
|--------------|-----------|
| Leafy greens | Same day |

| Specialty | 2-3 days |

## Quality Standards

### Produce Requirements
```
Grade A standard:
- Fresh appearance
- Proper ripeness
- No damage/bruising
- Consistent sizing
- Clean and trimmed
```

### Rejection Criteria
```
Reject if:
- Wilted or damaged
- Wrong ripeness
- Pest evidence
- Short dated
- Below grade
```

## Local Farm Relations

### Partnership Approach
```
Benefits of local:
- Fresher product
- Story for marketing
- Flexible ordering
- Community support

Trade-offs:
- Higher cost sometimes
- Limited variety
- Weather dependent
```

### Farm Visits
```
Quarterly visits:
- Quality at source
- Growing practices
- Relationship building
- Future planning
```
