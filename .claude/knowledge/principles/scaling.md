# Scaling Principles

*Source: Sam Altman - Startup Playbook & YC Advice*

## Core Scaling Philosophy

### Do Things That Don't Scale (First)
- Manual processes help you learn
- Talk to every early customer
- Understand the problem deeply
- Then automate what works

### Focus Obsessively on Growth
- Revenue solves most problems
- Growth covers many mistakes
- Stagnation reveals all flaws
- Momentum attracts opportunities

### Move Fast
- Speed is a competitive advantage
- Fast iteration beats perfect planning
- Ship and iterate
- Bias toward action

## The Scaling Ladder

```
1. Manual
   └─→ Do it yourself, learn everything

2. Process
   └─→ Document what works

3. Delegate
   └─→ Hand off to people/agents

4. Automate
   └─→ Remove human from loop

5. Scale
   └─→ Grow without proportional effort
```

## When to Scale What

| Activity | Scale When | Keep Manual When |
|----------|------------|------------------|
| Customer support | Patterns clear, volume high | Learning product-market fit |
| Marketing | Channels proven, CAC known | Testing new channels |
| Operations | Process stable, exceptions rare | Frequent edge cases |
| Product | Core value proven | Still discovering what works |

## Growth Metrics That Matter

### Primary Metrics
- Revenue growth rate
- Customer growth rate
- Retention/churn
- Unit economics (LTV:CAC)

### Leading Indicators
- Trial signups → Conversions
- Website traffic → Purchase intent
- Email engagement → Future purchases
- Social mentions → Brand awareness

## Scaling Challenges

### 1. Things Break at Scale
What worked at small scale fails at large scale:
- Manual processes can't keep up
- Communication overhead explodes
- Quality slips without systems
- Technical debt compounds

**Solution:** Build systems before you need them

### 2. Hiring Doesn't Scale Linearly
Adding people adds complexity:
- Communication paths = n(n-1)/2
- Management overhead grows
- Culture dilutes

**Solution:** Automate before hiring

### 3. Customer Expectations Change
As you grow:
- Customers expect more
- Competition notices you
- Brand reputation matters more

**Solution:** Over-deliver consistently

## Scaling for Our Businesses

### BOO (11K products, marketplace)
- Challenge: Supplier sync at scale
- Solution: Automated sync, exception handling
- Focus: System reliability, not manual fixes

### Teelixir (Premium DTC)
- Challenge: Maintaining quality as volume grows
- Solution: Standardized processes, quality gates
- Focus: Brand experience consistency

### Elevate (B2B Wholesale)
- Challenge: Account management at scale
- Solution: Self-service + automated touch points
- Focus: High-value accounts get attention

### RHF (Local delivery)
- Challenge: Geographic expansion
- Solution: Prove model, then replicate
- Focus: Unit economics per zone

## The 80/20 of Scaling

Focus effort on:
- 20% of products that drive 80% of revenue
- 20% of customers that drive 80% of profit
- 20% of channels that drive 80% of growth
- 20% of features that drive 80% of value

## Scaling Checklist

Before scaling any process:

- [ ] Is this working manually? (proven value)
- [ ] Is this documented? (transferable knowledge)
- [ ] What breaks at 10x volume?
- [ ] What's the cost per unit at scale?
- [ ] Who monitors this at scale?
- [ ] What's the rollback plan?

## Common Scaling Mistakes

### 1. Scaling Too Early
- Haven't found product-market fit
- Unit economics don't work
- Process still changing frequently

### 2. Scaling Too Late
- Competitors gain ground
- Team burns out on manual work
- Technical debt accumulates

### 3. Scaling the Wrong Things
- Vanity metrics (users without revenue)
- Premature automation (unstable processes)
- Hiring before systems

For every initiative, ask:
